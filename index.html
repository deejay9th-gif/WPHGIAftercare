Polyps

Fleshy growths on the lining. Most are harmless (benign) but some (adenomas) are removed to prevent future bowel cancer.

Small pouches in the bowel wall. Very common. 'Diverticulitis' is when they become inflamed or infected.

Haemorrhoids

Swollen veins in the bottom (anus). Common cause of fresh red blood. Can be treated with banding.

IBD & IBS

IBD (e.g. Crohn's, Colitis) is chronic inflammation. IBS is a common sensitivity causing pain/bloating without damage.

Ulcerative Colitis

Inflammation and ulcers in the large bowel. Symptoms often include bloody diarrhoea and urgency.

Cell changes in the gullet from acid reflux. Increases risk of cancer slightly. Often monitored with regular endoscopies.

Crohn's Disease

A type of IBD causing chronic inflammation anywhere in the gut. Symptoms include pain, diarrhoea, and weight loss.

Part of the stomach squeezes up into the chest. Very common. Often causes acid reflux or heartburn.

Autoimmune reaction to gluten (wheat, barley, rye) damaging the gut lining. Treated with a strict gluten-free diet.

Sores on the gut lining. Often caused by H. Pylori bacteria or anti-inflammatory painkillers (NSAIDs).

Review Your Report

Check your discharge report. Did the endoscopist mention any of the following?

Biopsy Samples

Samples have been sent to the lab. Results typically take 2‚Äì6 weeks. Your GP or consultant will contact you; please do not chase the endoscopy unit for results.

Polypectomy Clips

Small metal clips may have been used to prevent bleeding. These will fall off naturally and pass in your stool within 2 weeks. You likely won't notice them.

Throat Stretch (Dilatation)

Strict Diet Required:
‚Ä¢ 0-4 hrs: Cold fluids only.
‚Ä¢ 24 hrs: No hot drinks.
‚Ä¢ 3-5 days: Soft/Sloppy diet only.

Methylene Blue / Tattoo

Blue dye was used to highlight cells. Your urine or stool may turn blue/green for 24 hours. This is harmless and expected.

H. Pylori (CLO Test)

A test for stomach bacteria was performed. Results go to your GP in 1-2 weeks. If positive, you will need a course of antibiotics.

Banding of Piles

Rubber bands were used. A dull ache or feeling of needing to pass wind is common. Minor bleeding may occur when bands fall off (10-14 days).
